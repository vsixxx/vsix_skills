# Overlay: Industrial / Manufacturing

Use for manufacturing, processing, distribution-with-production, or asset-heavy industrial businesses where throughput, yield, uptime, customer concentration, and maintenance burden drive value.

## Decision lens
- Are plant throughput and backlog claims real and repeatable?
- Do yield, scrap, and downtime economics support the margin profile?
- Is revenue concentration or contract structure hiding downside?
- Is maintenance capex or environmental burden understated?

## Driver tree
Default equation:

`volume x realized price - material cost - direct labor - overhead - scrap/rework - maintenance burden`

## Mandatory denominators
- units produced, shipped, and booked
- realized price by product or customer bucket
- yield, scrap, and rework rates
- uptime, downtime, OEE, or line utilization
- backlog by customer and requested ship date
- on-time delivery and quality metrics
- labor hours per unit and overtime
- maintenance capex and plant repair history

## Typical gating items
- Throughput claims do not reconcile to line capacity, labor, or downtime.
- Margin relies on unusually low scrap, rework, or maintenance assumptions.
- Backlog quality is weak because orders are non-binding or concentrated.
- Environmental, safety, or permit issues could create cash or capex shocks.
- Customer concentration and pricing pressure are worse than presented.

## Kill criteria examples
- `hard pass`: normalized throughput or yield cannot support the required earnings level.
- `price reset`: maintenance capex, scrap, or labor normalization materially compresses margin.
- `pause`: unresolved environmental, safety, or quality issues create unknown liabilities.

## Evidence asks
- production, shipment, and backlog reports
- plant capacity and shift schedules
- scrap, yield, and rework logs
- downtime logs and maintenance work orders
- customer-level revenue and contract summaries
- quality metrics, returns, warranty, and claims history
- environmental permits, notices, and inspection records

## Owner mapping
- plant manager or operations lead
- controller or CFO
- maintenance manager
- quality lead
- EHS or compliance lead
- key equipment vendors

## External triangulation when thin
- environmental enforcement databases
- permit portals
- transportation or logistics context if freight is a major claim
- site and parcel records when expansion is part of the story

## Red flags
- Backlog is quoted without cancelability or customer concentration detail.
- EBITDA assumes little maintenance capex despite aging equipment.
- Yield or scrap is shown without a period-consistent production base.
- Quality or warranty costs are absent from the margin discussion.
