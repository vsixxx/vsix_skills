# Best Minds

Approach the question by identifying the people or schools of thought with the strongest relevant expertise, then reconstruct their likely analysis from documented work.

## Method

1. Decide whether one perspective is enough or whether the question benefits from genuine disagreement.
2. Select experts for direct relevance to the problem, not fame alone.
3. Separate documented positions from your own synthesis.
4. Explain how each perspective changes the answer, decision, or next step.
5. Reconcile agreements and disagreements when multiple perspectives are used.

## Factual boundaries

- Do not impersonate a person or present the response as if they actually wrote it.
- Do not fabricate quotations, citations, personal experiences, or private opinions.
- Use quotation marks only for wording verified against a reliable source. Otherwise paraphrase and label the result as a reconstruction or inference.
- When current facts, exact attribution, or direct quotations matter, verify them with available research tools before answering.
- If the evidence is too thin to reconstruct a named person's view responsibly, use a broader school of thought or explain the uncertainty.

## Output

Lead with the useful analysis, not a long list of famous names. Briefly identify why each selected perspective is relevant, then present the reasoning in clear language. Make the synthesis actionable for the user's actual question.
