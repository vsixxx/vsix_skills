# Writing Style Adaptation

Use this guide when adapting the voice of memos, decks, emails, research notes, IC papers, credit memos, board materials, or client deliverables.

## Preserve meaning before tone

A style rewrite must not alter the decision, claim, caveat, number, source, or degree of confidence unless the user requests substantive editing. If a sentence contains a factual claim or recommendation, rewrite only after identifying what must remain unchanged.

## Finance voice dimensions

Capture and apply the target style across these dimensions:

| Dimension | Options to infer |
|---|---|
| Headline style | descriptive label, so-what sentence, recommendation-first, question-led, issue-led |
| Tone | neutral, skeptical, commercial, board-level, banker-polished, research-analytical, lender-conservative |
| Evidence posture | source-forward, assumption-forward, management-claim separated, citation-light, footnote-heavy |
| Density | executive concise, MD-style dense, memo narrative, appendix-heavy, slide-fragmented |
| Assertion level | tentative, balanced, decisive, investment-view-forward, compliance-constrained |
| Bullet grammar | fragments, complete sentences, bold lead-ins, em dash structure, no punctuation, full punctuation |
| Caveat style | inline caveat, risk section, assumptions box, footnote, appendix limitation |

## Common target styles

### Investment banking deck

- Make page titles action-oriented and client-relevant.
- Use crisp bullets, parallel phrasing, and commercial implications.
- Use qualifiers carefully: avoid overstating if diligence is incomplete.
- Keep detail in exhibits/footnotes rather than long prose blocks.
- Prefer terms like supports, implies, highlights, suggests, indicates, creates opportunity, and positions when evidence supports them.

### Private equity / IC memo

- Lead with recommendation, thesis, key risks, diligence status, and returns/downside implications.
- Distinguish base case, downside, and diligence-to-date.
- Be skeptical of seller/management claims.
- Use concise but explicit risk language.

### Private credit / lender memo

- Prioritize downside, cash flow durability, debt service, collateral, covenant headroom, liquidity, and sponsor support.
- Use conservative language and avoid promotional phrasing.
- Separate borrower claims from lender analysis.

### Public markets research

- Separate reported facts, consensus/estimate changes, thesis implications, and variant view.
- Use precise catalyst, risk, and valuation language.
- Avoid investment advice overstatement when compliance posture is unknown.

### CFO / board / corporate finance

- Lead with business implication and decision needed.
- Use plain-language explanations tied to budget, forecast, variance, KPI, and operating actions.
- Keep financial detail scannable and action-oriented.

## Rewrite protocol

1. Identify source claims, numbers, and recommendations that cannot change.
2. Infer the target voice from the strongest style source.
3. Rewrite titles, bullets, paragraphs, and callouts using the target voice.
4. Compare the rewrite against the original meaning.
5. Flag any substantive changes separately from tone/formatting changes.

## Before/after format

For advice or QC tasks, use:

| Location | Original | Styled version | Change type | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Change type should be one of: tone, clarity, title, structure, caveat, citation, formatting, or substantive. Do not hide substantive edits under tone.
