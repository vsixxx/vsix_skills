# Evidence Hierarchy for Investment Banking and Finance

Use the hierarchy that fits the claim. The best source is the source closest to the claim, not always the newest or longest document.

## General ranking rules

1. Prefer primary sources over secondary summaries.
2. Prefer executed, filed, audited, signed, or system-of-record sources over presentations, emails, and verbal claims.
3. Prefer current-period sources over older sources unless the user requests historical analysis.
4. Prefer source-specific documents over broad marketing materials. For covenant terms, use the credit agreement. For actual revenue, use financial statements or ledger support. For management claims, use the transcript or meeting note and label it as a claim.
5. Do not rely on a lower-tier source when a higher-tier source is available and directly addresses the claim.
6. When the highest-tier source is unavailable, use lower-tier sources but label the evidence posture.

## Public-company equity and valuation work

Use this for comps, DCF, capital-markets updates, investment memos, public-company model updates, and market-event analyses.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Filed annual reports and audited financial statements: 10-K, 20-F, annual report, audited notes | Historical audited financials, segment data, risks, accounting policies, debt notes | Best source for annual historical financials; may be stale for current events |
| 1 | Filed current or quarterly reports: 10-Q, 6-K, 8-K, prospectus, S-1, F-1, proxy, merger proxy | Quarterly financials, current events, acquisitions, management changes, securities issuance, governance | Use current-event filings for event-specific facts |
| 2 | Earnings release, supplemental, investor presentation posted by company | KPI detail, non-GAAP reconciliations, management framing, guidance | Company controlled; reconcile with filings when filings are available |
| 2 | Earnings call transcript or company-hosted call | Management quotes, guidance explanation, Q&A, qualitative claims | Quote carefully; distinguish statement from your interpretation |
| 3 | Reliable market data or financial data vendor | Price, volume, consensus, estimates, multiples, financial data pulls | Always include vendor and as-of timestamp/date |
| 3 | Sell-side research or third-party expert reports | Consensus debate, variant views, market narrative | Secondary source; do not treat as proof of company facts |
| 4 | News media, blogs, social, unsourced web summaries | Event awareness, sentiment, early signals | Use only with caveats and corroborate before using in decisions |
| 5 | User-provided unsourced assumptions | Scenarios, placeholders, what-if analysis | Label as assumption and show sensitivity |

## Private equity, growth equity, and CorpDev diligence

Use this for CIM teardown, deal screening, QoE, LBO, investment memo, and portfolio diligence.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executed transaction documents, purchase agreements, signed contracts, board materials, audited statements | Legal economics, ownership, contractual terms, historical audited results | Highest value when signed/current |
| 1 | Trial balance, general ledger, bank statements, tax returns, payroll records, customer invoices, customer contracts | QoE, revenue support, cash, working capital, customer concentration | Most useful for full diligence and QoE work |
| 2 | Quality of earnings report, third-party diligence report, customer calls, expert calls, market study | Adjusted EBITDA, working capital, diligence findings, market risk | Review scope, date, preparer, and limitations |
| 2 | Management accounts, monthly financials, KPI dashboards, pipeline data, CRM exports | Current performance, operating metrics, bookings, churn | Tie to GL/TB where possible |
| 3 | CIM, teaser, investor deck, lender presentation, management presentation | Seller claims, headline narrative, preliminary metrics | Treat as claims to test, not facts |
| 4 | Management interviews, banker commentary, sponsor notes | Qualitative explanation, diligence leads | Attribute and request support |
| 5 | Industry databases, web research, peer comps | Market sizing, industry context, benchmarks | Use as context unless directly sourced and current |

## Private credit and leveraged finance

Use this for private-credit underwriting, debt capacity, covenant analysis, ABL, LBO debt sizing, credit memos, and portfolio monitoring.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executed credit agreement, indenture, note purchase agreement, intercreditor agreement, security agreement | Covenants, EBITDA definitions, baskets, liens, guarantees, priority, maturity | Do not rely on summaries for covenant math |
| 1 | Compliance certificate, borrowing base certificate, collateral schedules, field exam, appraisal | Covenant status, collateral, advance rates, borrowing availability | Must capture as-of date |
| 1 | Audited financials, QoE, monthly management accounts tied to source records | EBITDA, cash flow, leverage, liquidity, working capital | Separate management add-backs from diligence-supported add-backs |
| 2 | Rating agency reports, lender presentations, bank books | Market framing, credit strengths/risks, comparable credits | Secondary; verify core terms against docs |
| 3 | Market data vendor, trade levels, new issue data, LCD-like summaries, pricing indications | Spread, yield, OID, secondary price, issuance context | Timestamp market data |
| 4 | News, sponsor commentary, broker notes | Early warning signals, reputation, market sentiment | Corroborate before underwriting conclusion |

## Fixed income, rates, macro, FX, and commodities

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Central bank releases, government statistical agencies, treasury/finance ministry data, regulator releases | Policy rates, inflation, labor data, GDP, auction results, debt issuance, official reserves | Record release time, revision status, and period |
| 1 | Exchange, clearinghouse, index provider, issuer, or official curve source | Contract specs, settlement data, index levels, bond terms | Use exact instrument identifiers when possible |
| 2 | Market data vendor or terminal pull | Prices, yields, curves, spreads, vol, FX, commodities | Timestamp and define units/conventions |
| 3 | Bank strategy research, economist notes, market commentary | Interpretation, scenarios, positioning | Treat as opinion, not official data |
| 4 | News and social media | Event detection, color, early reactions | Confirm with primary or market data before conclusion |

## Real estate and real assets

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executed leases, rent roll, loan agreement, title, appraisal, environmental report, property financials, tax bills | NOI, occupancy, rollover, debt terms, collateral, property risks | Check property/date coverage |
| 1 | Bank statements, CAM reconciliations, invoices, capex records, utility bills | Cash receipts, expenses, capex, recoveries | Needed for operating due diligence |
| 2 | Broker package, OM, property management reports, budget, leasing pipeline | Seller claims, leasing strategy, capex plan | Treat as claims until tied to records |
| 2 | Market data vendor, public records, comparable sales/leases | Cap rates, rent comps, supply pipeline, local market context | Capture geography, asset type, and date |
| 3 | News, broker commentary, third-party articles | Market color | Use only as context unless corroborated |

## Distressed debt and restructuring

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Credit agreements, indentures, collateral documents, intercreditor agreements, court filings, plan documents, disclosure statement | Claim priority, liens, maturities, covenants, recovery waterfall, legal process | Cite exact provision or filing where possible |
| 1 | Debtor financials, liquidity reports, DIP budget, cash collateral order, 13-week cash flow | Liquidity, cash burn, DIP need, plan feasibility | Check period and court-filed status |
| 2 | Creditor presentations, valuation reports, expert reports | Recovery views, valuation disputes, stakeholder arguments | Attribute to party and test assumptions |
| 3 | Trade levels, pricing services, news, docket summaries | Market-implied recovery, event timeline | Verify with docket or primary documents before conclusion |

## Source override rule

If a lower-tier source is more directly relevant to the exact claim than a higher-tier source, explain the override. Example: an investor presentation may be a better source for management's stated long-term target, while the 10-K is a better source for historical audited revenue.

## Unsupported materiality rule

If a claim affects valuation, credit approval, deal continuation, risk rating, or investment recommendation and lacks adequate support, do not bury it in prose. Surface it as an evidence gap or diligence ask.
