## 1. Management or founder diligence call
Objective: test the company story, management credibility, KPIs, quality of growth, risks, and what evidence is needed next.

Must know:
- business model, product, customer base, market, unit economics, revenue quality, margin profile, cash flow, capex, working capital, and management incentives;
- what the model or memo assumes;
- seller or management claims that need verification.

Question focus:
- growth drivers and sustainability;
- customer concentration, churn, pipeline, pricing, retention, cohort behavior;
- gross margin and operating leverage drivers;
- non-recurring revenue, add-backs, revenue recognition, backlog, bookings, ARR/MRR, NRR/GRR as relevant;
- cash conversion, working capital, capex, debt, covenants, liquidity;
- what management would cut first in a downside case.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `cim-teardown`, `financials-normalizer`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `three-statement-model-builder`, `lbo-model-build`.

## 2. Banker pitch or client update
Objective: prepare the user to advise, win trust, advance a process, or clarify next steps with a client.

Must know:
- client objective, current mandate status, decision-maker, process timeline, known sensitivities;
- relevant comps, precedent transactions, valuation range, market window, buyer/lender/investor universe;
- what the user should ask for to sharpen the pitch or process.

Question focus:
- strategic objective and constraints;
- valuation expectations and minimum acceptable outcome;
- buyer/lender/investor preferences;
- timing, approvals, confidentiality, and process risks;
- materials needed to launch or refresh process.

Useful companion skills: `pitch-deck-builder`, `ib-deck-qc`, `buyer-investor-list`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `private-credit-underwriting`, `covenant-package-analyzer`, `capital-markets-issuance`, `deal-process-tracker`, `style-guide-adapter`.

## 3. Buyer, sponsor, or investor meeting
Objective: help the user qualify interest, understand underwriting constraints, and identify what would move a counterparty toward action.

Must know:
- counterparty mandate, prior behavior, sector focus, deal size, check size, financing appetite, valuation sensitivity;
- why this opportunity fits or does not fit;
- likely diligence objections and decision process.

Question focus:
- investment mandate and non-negotiables;
- valuation, leverage, growth, margin, synergy, and exit assumptions;
- diligence priorities and timeline;
- committee process and gating concerns;
- reasons they might pass.

Useful companion skills: `buyer-investor-list`, `lbo-model-build`, `private-credit-underwriting`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `deal-process-tracker`.

## 4. Lender or credit committee meeting
Objective: pressure-test repayment capacity, downside protection, structure, collateral, covenants, liquidity, and lender asks.

Must know:
- capital structure, debt terms, maturity wall, collateral, covenant package, liquidity, cash burn, forecast, lender case;
- EBITDA adjustments, add-backs, NWC, capex, cash taxes, debt capacity, and recovery downside;
- sponsor support and covenant headroom.

Question focus:
- what protects lenders in the downside;
- covenant setting and cushion;
- liquidity runway and revolver usage;
- add-back support and recurring EBITDA;
- collateral and recovery value;
- reporting package and ongoing monitoring.

Useful companion skills: `private-credit-underwriting`, `covenant-package-analyzer`, `lbo-model-build`, `financials-normalizer`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `distressed-recovery-waterfall`.

## 5. Investment committee or internal deal review
Objective: prepare the user to make or defend a recommendation, expose the decision needed, and resolve the highest-impact open questions.

Must know:
- thesis, variant view, key risks, valuation, returns, downside, catalysts, diligence status, kill criteria;
- what has changed since last review;
- what the committee must decide today.

Question focus:
- what has to be true to win;
- biggest disconfirming evidence;
- downside case and breakeven points;
- valuation sensitivity and exit assumptions;
- diligence gaps and who owns them;
- go/no-go recommendation.

Useful companion skills: `memo-builder`, `cim-teardown`, `lbo-model-build`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `model-audit-tieout`.

## 6. Public-company client, investor day, or market-update meeting
Objective: prepare for a public-company client meeting, investor day, sell-side conference, or market-update discussion in an IB context.

Must know:
- latest print, guidance, consensus, KPIs, transcript commentary, estimate revisions, valuation, debate points, catalysts;
- what the market expected versus what changed;
- what question would test the variant view.

For an introductory public-company coverage meeting, treat the goal as earning informed follow-up rather than proving a mandate exists. Lead with the public-source strategic and capital-allocation read, identify potential mandate triggers, and use one compact question sequence. Keep detailed diligence branches secondary until management signals an actionable priority or agrees to follow-up work.

Question focus:
- guidance bridge and key drivers;
- demand, pricing, volume, mix, margin, capex, cash flow, buybacks, balance sheet;
- KPI definitions and comparability;
- competitive dynamics and channel signals;
- risks management is not emphasizing;
- what would cause estimates to move.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `financial-source-of-truth`, `financials-normalizer`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `capital-markets-issuance`, `pitch-deck-builder`.

## 7. Expert call
Objective: turn limited expert time into specific evidence on market structure, competitive position, customer behavior, operational reality, or industry change.

Must know:
- expert background and potential bias;
- hypotheses to test;
- what evidence is observable by the expert versus outside their scope;
- compliance restrictions and topics to avoid.

Question focus:
- observable facts and examples rather than opinions;
- triangulation against company claims;
- customer behavior, switching costs, procurement, pricing, sales cycles, churn, channel dynamics;
- competitor positioning;
- leading indicators and inflection points.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `buyer-investor-list`, `comps-valuation`, `cim-teardown`, `memo-builder`.

## 8. Portfolio company operating review
Objective: identify what changed in performance, what actions are needed, and what leadership should do next.

Must know:
- budget versus actuals, forecast, KPI trend, cash/liquidity, covenants, hiring, sales pipeline, initiatives, risks;
- prior action items and ownership;
- management explanations and data quality issues.

Question focus:
- misses versus plan and root causes;
- revised forecast and confidence;
- initiatives, blockers, owners, and timing;
- cash, working capital, hiring, capex, procurement, pricing;
- board-level decisions needed.

Useful companion skills: `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `model-audit-tieout`, `memo-builder`, `three-statement-model-builder`, `private-credit-underwriting`.

## 9. CFO, budget, forecast, or board meeting
Objective: prepare finance leadership for decisions on budget, forecast, resource allocation, financial performance, or board communication.

Must know:
- current plan, actuals, forecast, variance drivers, asks, constraints, targets, approvals;
- what changed since prior forecast or board package;
- what decisions are needed and by when.

Question focus:
- source of variance and controllability;
- recurring versus one-time impact;
- tradeoffs, ROI, payback, hiring plan, vendor spend, budget ownership;
- forecast confidence and downside plan;
- decisions, approvals, and escalation path.

Useful companion skills: `budget-intake-challenger`, `financials-normalizer`, `excel-data-cleaner`, `model-audit-tieout`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `memo-builder`, `ib-deck-qc`.

## 10. Deal process or diligence kickoff
Objective: align on process, responsibilities, gating questions, timeline, workstreams, and evidence needed.

Must know:
- process stage, buyer/lender/investor list, NDA status, deadlines, diligence workstreams, data room readiness, open issues;
- who owns each workstream;
- next milestone and decision gate.

Question focus:
- timeline and deliverables;
- owners and dependencies;
- top deal-breakers and workstream priorities;
- data room gaps;
- approval process and escalation;
- communication cadence.

Useful companion skills: `deal-process-tracker`, `cim-teardown`, `financials-normalizer`, `buyer-investor-list`, `memo-builder`.
