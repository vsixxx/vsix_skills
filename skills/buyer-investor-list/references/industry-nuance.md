## Software and technology

Look beyond "software" tags. Consider:

- Vertical vs horizontal software.
- ARR, revenue retention, gross retention, net retention, churn, cac payback, rule of 40, gross margin, implementation burden.
- Product adjacency, platform vs point solution, api/integration fit, data sensitivity, security/compliance posture.
- Enterprise vs smb vs mid-market customer base.
- Sponsor-backed platform add-on logic vs new platform investment.
- Strategic buyer build-vs-buy posture.

Senior judgment:

- The best strategic may be an adjacent platform with a product gap, not the largest direct competitor.
- Sponsor-backed platforms are often faster but may have valuation ceilings.
- Growth investors need confidence in retention, sales efficiency, and durable growth, not just headline arr growth.

## Healthcare

Consider:

- Provider, payer, hc it, pharma services, medtech, life sciences tools, distribution, outsourcing.
- Reimbursement, cms/medicaid/medicare, payor mix, licensing, compliance, quality metrics, clinical risk.
- Local market share, certificate-of-need dynamics, physician ownership, state approvals.
- Sponsor specialization and operating expertise.

Senior judgment:

- Regulatory and reimbursement comfort can outrank headline sector fit.
- Local density often matters more than national scale for provider services.
- Direct strategic buyers may be logical but require careful confidentiality and antitrust analysis.

## Industrials and business services

Consider:

- Route density, branch network, geographic overlap, recurring maintenance vs project revenue.
- Labor intensity, union exposure, safety record, fleet/capex, working capital, procurement synergies.
- Customer concentration, contract duration, cyclicality, and regional consolidation.

Senior judgment:

- Synergy value often comes from density, route overlap, procurement, and branch consolidation.
- Family offices or long-hold sponsors may be credible for founder-owned services assets.

## Consumer and retail

Consider:

- Brand fit, channel mix, d2c vs wholesale, retailer relationships, demographic overlap.
- Gross margin, inventory, marketing efficiency, repeat purchase, category trend, international white space.
- Working capital and seasonality.

Senior judgment:

- Strategic buyers may value distribution or category gap, but sponsors will underwrite repeatability and inventory discipline.
- Trend/fad categories need extra skepticism and stronger evidence of durability.

## Energy, infrastructure, and real assets

Consider:

- Contracted cash flows, counterparty quality, commodity exposure, asset life, maintenance capex, permitting, environmental liabilities.
- Inflation linkage, tax credits/subsidies, regulatory framework, project finance suitability.

Senior judgment:

- Infrastructure funds may outbid traditional pe for long-duration contracted cash flows.
- Environmental, permitting, and regulatory issues can dominate fit.

## Financial institutions and fintech

Consider:

- Licenses, bank partnerships, aml/kyc, fraud, credit risk, compliance, deposit/funding model, capital requirements.
- Customer data sensitivity and regulator relationships.

Senior judgment:

- A buyer's regulatory permission and risk appetite may matter more than product fit.
- Banks can be logical but slow and diligence-heavy.

## Distressed and restructuring

Consider:

- Existing creditor position, lien priority, collateral, liquidity runway, maturity wall, recovery, plan path, bankruptcy sale mechanics.
- Dip, exit financing, credit bidding, rescue capital, stalking-horse role, litigation appetite.

Senior judgment:

- The best party may be an existing creditor or capital provider, not a strategic buyer.
- Speed, legal sophistication, and certainty can matter more than nominal headline value.

## Cross-border buyers

Consider:

- Foreign investment approvals, sanctions, currency, financing, governance, cultural integration, political risk, data transfer, tax, local labor law.

Senior judgment:

- Cross-border buyers can create valuation upside but should often be sequenced after a domestic baseline unless they are a clear must-call.

## Sponsor-specific judgment

Score sponsors on:

- Mandate, fund vintage, likely capacity, check size, sector experience, platform/add-on logic, relationship access, ic speed, and reputation.
- Control vs minority, founder-friendliness, carve-out capability, operating resources, leverage appetite.

Do not equate aum with dry powder. Do not assume a fund can invest merely because it is large.

## Strategic-buyer-specific judgment

Score strategics on:

- Strategic gap, synergy potential, balance sheet, public strategy, acquisition history, integration capacity, confidentiality, regulatory risk, and internal build alternatives.

The key question is: why would the buyer's ceo or business-unit leader care now?

## Lender-specific judgment

Score lenders on:

- Facility fit, hold size, lead capability, sector appetite, leverage/pricing/covenant posture, documentation speed, sponsored/non-sponsored appetite, existing relationship, and downside tolerance.

A lender that cannot lead may still be useful as a participant but should not be tier 1 lead.
